NYC Citi Bike & Taxi Analytics

Analyzed New York City's public bike sharing system "Citi Bike" along with yellow and green taxi datasets using Hadoop and Pig. Aiming to improve the usage of Citi Bikes, our algorithm aggregated data from millions of taxi and bike rides over the last 7 years to guide users which mode of transport to choose. This was based on user's source and destination, taxi ride time, taxi fare, bike ride time, proximity of Citi Bike docking stations, etc. Also, applied K-means clustering using Spark to suggest locations where new Citi Bike stations can be added.

Movie Search Engine

Developed a scalable, personalized search engine model for movies and actors with an average latency of 0.4 seconds. A total of 14845 movies and 229830 actors were indexed from a corpus generated by crawling IMDb and Wikipedia pages. An intelligent agent was provided to assist users during their search session. It corrected typographical errors using Levenshtein distance and Jaro–Winkler word similarity algorithms. The agent also helped in improving user intent for queries that derived poor search results. It recommended similar queries based on the user's previous searches and the proximity to other queries in the movies and actors content association graph.

OurBlock

Developed a web app which was primarily aimed to figure out the real-time occupancy status of open spaces in the office building. Motion detectors and sound sensors were installed at multiple locations and data from those sensors was used to display the real-time status of conference rooms on a map of the building. The app can then be used for 1-click booking of the conference rooms on the fly. Used Leaflet JS library to render the building map and AJAX to load the data from the sensors which was displayed on a front-end built on AngularJS and Bootstrap. The app had a responsive design and could be accessed via touchscreen kiosks installed in the office as well as on smartphones.
